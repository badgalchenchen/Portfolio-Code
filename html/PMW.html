MY ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES

To date, no PMW has yet to be successfully kept in captivity due to its fragility, and there are limited online and offline research. I was in-charge of the sketches of the initial ideas and also some of the preliminary prototyping and testings for the project. I learnt a lot about project management and work distribution as we have limited time for the project. When testing prototypes, I also learnt how to test quantitatively to hit specifications. This project gave me a sneak peak of what working as a design engineer in a real company feels like, and I am grateful for this opportunity.

PROCESS

PRELIMINARY RESEARCH: LEARNING ABOUT THE PMW

As there were little primary research, we searched the internet for experts on invertebrates and tried to find the contacts of the researchers who have tried to keep the PMW in captivitiy. After talking to a few experts, we managed to conclude that there are 2 main design requirements that determines the PMW's survival.

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS: WIND AND KEEPING OFF TANK WALLS

It must help the PMW avoid the tank walls. When the PMW hits any surfaces, it may bruise and die.

DEFINING THE DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

The intial idea consists of 5 rollers with a porous fabric wrapped around them. This provides
- Body conforming fabric
- Height change
- Assisted turning mechanism (see slide show)

ITERATION: VISIT TO WOODLANDS CAREHOME

At the carehome, the caregivers demonstrated the process they use to shower the residents. We got to put ourselves in the shoes of the caregivers and the residents. We realised several problems with The initial Xpod which includes:
- Transfer: It is difficult to transfer the resident from their beds to The Pod V.1
- Size: it is too bulky and heavy to navigate through narrow corridors with ease
- Cost: It is probably too expensive to be built
- Safety: There are too many exposed electrical parts.

ITERATION AND FINAL DESIGN: BETTERING THE DESIGN

We were given $1000 to build our prototype. After brainstorming and sketching sessions, we decided to streamline the design and make it simpler and more user friendly.

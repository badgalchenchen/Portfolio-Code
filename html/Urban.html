Design and develop the site

The site was located near United Center, Near West Side, Chicago

Duration

Jan 2019 to Mar 2019

Role

Lead Urban Designer

MY ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES

As I was the only one who had Design and Architecture 3D modelling (Revit) experience, I was leading most of the design decisions and also the technical modelling of the site. During the brainstorming session, I took everyone's individual Masterplan and further developed them with the team, combining them to form the Final Plan, The Turtle. I also contributed to most of the graphic design work done to better bring our point across to the panel. Urban design requires a lot of thoughtful thinking, and we tried to make the best experience for the residents and visitors of our site, increasing interconnectivity and urban interactions.

MAIN IDEA

SITE ANALYSIS

I went down to the site itself and took a walk, taking note of its surroundings and vibes. Here are some of my findings:

1. United Center is surrounded by large empty areas of just carparks with no interactive urban spaces. Hence, the site is only vibrant when there is a game in the United Center

2. Surrounded by low to mid income housing, the site seems scary to walk through

3. Besides the United Center, other areas on the site are mostly occupied by residential buildings, schools and churches

4. Picture 2 shows a radial walk map, which indicates an empty space which is not reachable by 10min walking from the nearest train station. It is a potential area for a train station.

PERSONAL INITIAL IDEA

Yellow: Residential
Blue: Business area
Purple: Community space
Green: Green space
Red: Retail zone

This initial master plan focuses on keeping the Untied Center as a central node where all main roads meet. It also emphasises on making the whole site more walkable and vibrant. It makes use of previously unused space such as areas under the train tracks, and also a luxury retail walkway from the train station to the United Center. By adding more retail, green and community spaces, the site is kept active even after game time, ensuring vibrancy even when dark.

IMPROVEMENTS NEEDED: FOCUS ON OVERALL THEME, TRAFFIC FLOW, SUN PATH

After many critique sessions and drawing many different ideas on the wad of tracing paper, we realised that we have problems in the following areas
1. OVERALL THEME: It was not clear what was the main idea behind our masterplan
2. TRAFFIC FLOW: The traffic that loops around the United Center may cause more physical separation between the United Center and the public spaces, as it will be at least a 4 lane road.
3. SUN PATH: We need to know where to place our taller skyscrapers as they will be casting a shadow on the other areas. Also, we need to ensure that we maximise the morning sun and avoid the afternoon sun as much as possible.

FINAL MASTERPLAN: THE TURTLE

The final concept, The Turtle, places United Center in the limelight. We realised the importance of Madison road which goes past the United Center. It brings high amounts of traffic from the suburbs to Chicago city. Hence, we decided to keep the road. However, the road adds an abrupt edge to United Center. To add more interactive spaces at United Center, we added a plaza space infront and behind the building.

Branching off from the center of The Turtle are 6 different districts, namely Shopping, Green Space, Cultural, Business, Sports, and Recreational

DESIGN PROCESS

We wanted to bring vibrancy to United Center by changing up the traffic flow around it. This allows United Center to be included in the hustle and bustle of the other areas. This ensures that the area is still kept active even after dark and when there is no games. We chose to reroute the road on the south side as the north area is kept as a walkeable

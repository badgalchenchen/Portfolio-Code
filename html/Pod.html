The Pod, an assisted bathing solution

A device to help caregivers bathe an elderly resident with ease.

Client:Ministry of Health (MOH), Woodlands Care Home

s

Problem given

How might we make it easier for caregivers to shower elderly residents who are not physically able to support themselves?

Duration

Aug 2017 to Nov 2017

Role

Design Engineer

MY ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES

My team was given a very broad problem with a million different ways we can tackle them. In order to narrow down the problem areas, I led a discussion session where we mapped out the users' journey and their pain points. We decided to focus on reducing the manpower needed from 2 to 1 caregiver. Next, my team brainstormed for ideas and using the quick sketch, I helped in the 3D modelling of the design using Solidworks. After visiting the carehome and realising problems with our design, we worked on the second iteration of the design which is a lot simpler and less electronics heavy. Lastly, I was in-charge of building the hands-free showerhead mount. It was a challenging protoype to build but we did a good job in finding work arounds for difficult mechanisms, especially with a tight budget.

FINAL DESIGN: XPOD

PROCESS

EMPATHISING: USER JOURNEY MAPPING

1. Manpower intensive: requires 2 caregivers to shower 1 resident due to heavy lifting
2. Residents may feel humiliated
3. Difficult to transfer resident from their beds to the assisted bathing device

DEFINING THE PROBLEM: REDUCING MANPOWER

How might we reduce the manpower it takes to bathe an elderly from 2 to 1 while maintaining the humility of the resident?

INITIAL BRAINSTORMING IDEA: 5 ROLLER SYSTEM

The intial idea consists of 5 rollers with a porous fabric wrapped around them. This provides
- Body conforming fabric
- Height change
- Assisted turning mechanism (see slide show)

ITERATION: VISIT TO WOODLANDS CAREHOME

At the carehome, the caregivers demonstrated the process they use to shower the residents. We got to put ourselves in the shoes of the caregivers and the residents. We realised several problems with The initial Xpod which includes:
- Transfer: It is difficult to transfer the resident from their beds to The Pod V.1
- Size: it is too bulky and heavy to navigate through narrow corridors with ease
- Cost: It is probably too expensive to be built
- Safety: There are too many exposed electrical parts.

ITERATION AND FINAL DESIGN: BETTERING THE DESIGN

We were given $1000 to build our prototype. After brainstorming and sketching sessions, we decided to streamline the design and make it simpler and more user friendly.
